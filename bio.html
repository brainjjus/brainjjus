About

Ewan Knight is a UK-based musician, composer, and sound designer whose work spans ambient music, experimental performance, and collaborative sound art. Working under the artist name brainjjus, he creates emotionally resonant solo ambient works that explore inner landscapes, psychological states, and the act of listening. His debut EP you might never be in the same room you are listening to this again is due for release in late 2025.

In 2025, he premiered Things Fall Between, a solo performance and forthcoming album structured in six movements. Performed live with synthesizers, guitar, and effects in an immersive setup, the work examines the metaphor of falling off a cliff as a psychological rupture. Drawing from influences such as Arvo Pärt, Hiroshi Yoshimura, and Patricia Wolf, it represents a culmination of Ewan’s compositional, performance, and technical craft.

Ewan is one half of Individual Hotpot, a collaborative duo with Korean artist Nam Joo Huh. Together, they developed Gut Feelings, a semi-improvised performance lecture on sonic shame and involuntary bodily sound. The piece has been presented in galleries and experimental spaces across the UK and Europe and is currently evolving into a large-scale immersive exhibition combining field recordings, live electronics, spoken word, and participatory elements.

Alongside his creative work, Ewan is an experienced session and dep musician, having performed with artists including Selina Lee and Mat Hook. His work as a live instrumentalist has taken him across the UK and internationally, contributing to projects across genres and settings.

He was recently selected for the Earthsonic Futures programme, supporting emerging artists working with sound and environmental activism.

Ewan is a classically trained guitarist and multi-instrumentalist. He is also a dedicated educator, teaching through Sheffield Music Hub, Sheffield Inclusion Centre, and privately. He focuses on inclusive approaches to performance, composition, and music technology.
